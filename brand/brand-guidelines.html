# brand guidelines — v1

Ink discipline, one accent.

This is the first written version of the Cognitidata brand — before this, it lived as scattered decisions across the site's CSS and a designer's memory. One neutral system, one signal color, used the same way everywhere it appears.

v1.0 · 2026 · Applies to: cognitidata.com, social/carousel content, client-facing collateral

01

Logo

The mark is a literal drawing of what the company does: a dashed line — the broken, manual way of working — resolves into a solid line and a dot. Interrupted, then fixed. Keep that story intact; don't simplify it into an abstract shape.

  • — The full lockup (mark + wordmark) is for the site header, footer, and anywhere the brand needs a name attached. The mark alone is for favicons, app icons, and social profile photos — anywhere space is tight.
  • — Mark and wordmark: always ink (#111111) on a light ground, or reversed to the paper color on a dark/ink ground. Never in the accent color. The one exception is "AI" in the "AI Solutions" tagline, which carries the accent deliberately — everything else in the lockup stays neutral so that one word keeps its meaning as a signal.
  • — Minimum clearspace around the mark: the height of the dot, on all sides. Don't crowd it with text or edges.

02

Color

Two layers: a neutral foundation that does almost all the work, and one accent — Nord Frost — that appears in exactly three places: emphasis words, buttons/links, and index numbers. Nothing else carries color.

Foundation

Page#FAFAF9Background, everywhere — near-white, not flat grey
Surface#FFFFFFCards, panels above the page
Ink#111111Headlines, primary text, logo
Body#444444Paragraph copy
Hint#999999Eyebrows, nav, muted labels
Border#DCDCDCDividers, card outlines

Accent — Nord Frost

Frost Deep#3F6C7AEmphasis words, buttons, links, code keywords
Frost Soft#7FA8B5Index numbers, quiet decorative marks
Frost Fill#3F6C7AOne full-color moment: end cards, closing CTAs
The rule that matters most: the accent is a signal, not a decoration. If everything is accented, nothing is. Before adding a fourth touchpoint, ask whether an existing one already covers it.

03

Typography

One typeface, Montserrat, carrying every role through weight alone. No serif, no monospace — the earlier Cormorant Garamond / Outfit pairing and the Anton / Georgia carousel pairing are both retired in favor of this single family.

Headline Weight 700
Hero / section titles
Emphasis Weight 700, italic
The one accented word per headline
Subhead / label Weight 600
Card titles, eyebrows, buttons, nav
Body copy sits at weight 500, never lighter Weight 500
Paragraph text
Why no weight below 500: at Montserrat's proportions, anything lighter reads thin and undermines legibility — this was a direct fix after an earlier pass felt too delicate to read comfortably.

04

Voice

Drawn from the actual copy already live on the site — this section describes a pattern that already exists, not a new invention. Treat it as observed, not prescribed; revisit once more copy exists to check it against.

  • Plain-spokenNo jargon, no framework names dropped for effect. Says what a thing does, not what category it belongs to.
  • Anti-fluff, on the recordStates what a thing is not as often as what it is — a habit that reads as confidence rather than hedging.
  • Evidence over adjectivesLeads with a number or a fact ("40+ systems shipped") instead of a claim ("industry-leading").

"No jargon, no slide decks."— How It Works

"First call is free, no pitch deck, no pressure."— CTA

"We train your team and make sure nothing feels like a black box."— How It Works

06

Don't

  • × Add a second accent color. If Frost isn't working somewhere, that's a signal to rethink the layout, not to introduce a new hue.
  • × Fill large areas with the accent outside the one established convention (end cards / closing CTAs).
  • × Use the accent as a colored border-left "strip" on cards or callouts. Borders stay neutral (#DCDCDC); the accent lives in text, buttons, and numbers only.
  • × Reintroduce serif or monospace type. Montserrat carries every role now, including code samples.
  • × Color the logo. It stays ink-only so the accent keeps its meaning.
  • × Use pure black (#000000) or pure white (#FFFFFF) as page background — the system runs on off-black ink and off-white paper, never true extremes.
